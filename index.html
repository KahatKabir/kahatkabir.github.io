Meet the Group

Prahlad Tipaniya - Artist

Padma Shree Prahlad Singh Tipanya is one of the most compelling folk voices in India today. Born in a small village in central India, he has earned international acclaim for his captivating music and the profound messages he shares through his performances. He specializes in singing and interpreting the verses of the 15th-century poet Kabir and other Indian mystical saint-poets. Every bhajan (song) Tipanya shares is a deep exploration into devotion (bhakti) and spiritual practice (yoga), all grounded within the realities of everyday life experience.

Devnarayan Saroliya - Artist

Devnarayan Saroliya, accompanying on violin and vocals, is an accomplished instrumentalist in many styles of Indian folk music. His use of violin to enhance the musical settings of mystical poetry has been influential throughout the entire Malwa region of India, inspiring many young artists to emulate his style. Although he is a capable improviser, while accompanying Prahlad ji he maintains a sensitive and subdued style that maintains a focus on the poetic structure of each piece.

Ajay Tipaniya - Artist

Ajay Tipanya, Prahlad ji's oldest son, will be accompanying him on dholak. As co-director of the Satguru Kabir Sansthan, Ajay has dedicated himself to helping his father share the poetry of Kabir throughout the world. Ajay's magic fingers imbue performances with a joy and energy fitting the spirit of the poetry itself, while his poetic sensitivity ensures that the rhythm always enhances the message rather than distracting from it.

Pranjali Sirasao - Coordinator & Translator

Pranjali Sirasao, co-coordinator of Tipaniyaji's 2018 US tour, is a faculty member at the University of California, Berkeley. She also hosts the extremely popular Chaitime radio show at Stanford University. Pranjali has organized dozens of cultural events in the Bay Area, presenting prominent South Asian artists, poets, and performers. Pranjali is the official bilingual translator for the 2018 tour.

Amba Devi - Coordinator

Ambda Caldwell, is a retired university professor, with a specialization in arts and religion of South Asia. She has taught at Harvard Divinity School, the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, and University of Calfiornia, Berkeley. Over the last year Amba has been residing at Tipaniyaji's ashram in rural Madhya Pradesh, learning the bhajans and studying spiritual practice. She is the co-founder of Akhil Vishwa Hindi Jyoti and board member of Mithila Art Institute.

Vivek Virani - Guest Artist

Vivek Virani is currently an Assistant Professor of ethnomusicology and music theory at the University of North Texas in Denton. His research addresses the importance of spiritual music and poetry in contemporary Indian society. While conducting fieldwork for his dissertation (UCLA 2016) he spent several months living with, learning from, and performing alongside Prahlad ji and the troupe.
